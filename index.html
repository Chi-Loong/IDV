26th Jan (Thurs)

Intro to data viz

Introduction. Course schedule and expectations. Why do data visualization? Historical examples. Exploratory vs. explanatory. A visualization analysis framework.

The big picture

Big picture questions. Why are computers and humans involved in data visualization? Why show the data? Why allow people to interact with it? What resource limitations are there? Also, course admin.

Week 2

2nd Feb (Thurs)

All about data

Dataset types — tables, charts, networks, fields, geometry, hiereachies. Data scales — nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio. Data ordering — sequential, diverging, cyclic.

HTML / CSS / JS

A refresher on web core technologies – HTML / CSS / JS. Useful JS design patterns that you will see over and over again. Function chaining, interfacing with backend systems with JSON and AJAX, dealing with asynchronous issues with callbacks and promises.

Task abstraction (why)

Ban Liang Ling (SUTD)

Datavis SG Meetup

Making architectural design decisions require the understanding and appreciation of various parameters.

The geometry of a building is often studied alongside performance maps, providing designers with a data-driven strategy.

Data visualisation in architectural design looks into this relationship, presenting interactive tools used during a design process.

Visualization validation

4 levels of visualization validation: domain/problem → data/task abstraction → viz idiom → algorithm / technology. Case studies and examples.

Marks, channels: Applied Perception

Defining how visualizations are composed of marks and channels. Analyzing the effectiveness of visual channels through accuracy, discriminability, salience, separability.

Intro to D3

Introduction to D3 (Data Driven Documents), the defacto data visualization library for the web. Toolchains and other libraries. Selections, domains, ranges, axes. Enter, update, exit - design pattern for glassy transitions.

Yan Nuang Oak (Thibi.co)

Datavis SG Meetup

Geospatial data visualisation is important for many kinds of impactful applications. However, geospatial data also comes with its own set of technologies and idiosyncrasies that makes it different from other kinds of data visualisation.

This talk will give an overview of the browser based technologies that are often used for interactive maps, using the process for building a biodiversity portal for Myanmar as a case study.

Bianchi, Samira (Kontinentalist)

Datavis SG Meetup

In this talk, award winning Singapore data visualization studio Kontinentalist will share their experience on how we use creative ways to source for and create their own data sets for their stories that involve mapping.

A huge part of why they are able to do that is through their work and engagement with community partners and audiences, who play an important role in their data storytelling process.

They’ll discuss how they work with these external partners to collect and create data stories in equitable, fair, and participatory ways, as well as do a brief run-through of their favourite mapping tools like Google Maps and Flourish.

Hanif Samad (Saltmine)

Datavis SG Meetup

There are many questions of public interest that would be well-informed by access to and analysis of relevant data. But what if the relevant data is inaccessible to the average citizen?

With the right tools, there are ways to get hold of data that will allow even a sole citizen to engage in a more meaningful and informed discussion of any issue.

In this session, Hanif will share a couple of personal and collaborative data projects motivated by a desire to raise the quality of discourse on broad-interest topics.

He will share the effort involved and how even a small measure of data, presented effectively, can cut through the noise of discourse.
